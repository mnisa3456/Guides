Part One

Choosing your niche

Why most beginners get this wrong

Most people choose a niche based on what they think will perform, not what they can sustain. They jump in, post for five weeks, and quit — because they never actually liked the content they were making.

Category vs. micro-niche

"Beauty" is not a niche. A micro-niche is specific enough that when someone lands on your page, they instantly know if it's for them.

  • Beauty → Skincare for South Asian skin tones — what works, what doesn't, and why
  • Fitness → Strength training for women over 40 starting from zero
  • Finance → Budgeting for first-generation immigrants in the West
Your micro-niche = category + specific audience + specific outcome. All three together.
Part Two

How the algorithm works

There is no single algorithm

There's a recommendation engine running continuous small tests on every post you publish. Think of it as gates — your content earns its way to larger audiences based on how each group responds.

What actually moves the needle
  • Saves — the strongest signal that your content has lasting value
  • Shares and DM sends — tells the system your content is worth spreading
  • Watch time and rewatches — especially full completion or looping
  • Comments — especially ones that spark conversation
  • Likes — real, but the weakest signal on this list
Most beginners optimise for likes. Likes are the weakest signal. Start thinking about saves first.
Part Three

Keywords and discoverability

Why this matters more than hashtags now

Most platforms now behave more like search engines — they understand what your content is about through your captions, on-screen text, spoken audio, and bio. Not just hashtags.

Where to place keywords
  • Your bio — searchable language describing who you help
  • First line of your caption — in words someone would actually type into search
  • Out loud in the first 5 seconds of your video
  • On-screen text and alt text (almost everyone skips this one)
The goal isn't to trick the algorithm — it's to make it as easy as possible to correctly categorise your content.
